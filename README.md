# ◈ MINDSCAPE DOS

*A first-person dungeon crawler through the Nuclear Twilight.*

Lead a party of four down through three worlds — the Underground, the Upper World, and Mt. Truth — gathering the seals, parleying with the strange beings of the MindScape, and breaking the final seal before the old world's last dream wakes.

A single file. No installs, no dependencies, no accounts. Open it in a browser and play.

*Adapted from the MindScape RPG by Luminosity — Matthew Kowalski.*

---

## Play

Open `index.html` in any modern browser — desktop, tablet, or phone. That's the whole game; everything runs locally, and your progress saves to your own browser.

On the title screen, choose your difficulty (**Explorer**, **Standard**, or **Hardcore**) and begin.

---

## The Quest

New Earth is locked under nuclear twilight. The way out lies beneath Mt. Truth, behind a door that answers only to a complete will.

Descend the three floors, recover the four seals the door demands, face what guards the last of them, and open MindScape. How you get there is up to you — the dungeon is wider, and kinder, and stranger than the straight path through it.

---

## Controls

**Movement**
- **W / ↑** — step forward · **S / ↓** — step back
- **A / ←** — turn left · **D / →** — turn right
- On touch screens, use the on-screen movement pad

**Actions**
- **Space** — Talk / Use (speak to a being, open a door, search where you stand)
- **L** — Look around
- **E** — Equipment

**In battle**, use the action buttons: **Attack**, **Heal**, **Zelio Blast**, **Force Field**, **Item**, and **Camp**.

Tap **Codex** any time to open the full MindScape reference — races, clans, skills, spells, mutant abilities, equipment, the creature archive, and the lore of New Earth.

## Features

- A party of four — **Zelio**, **Iron Jack**, **Moon Wolf**, and **No-See** — each with their own race, clan, skill, and spells
- Three hand-built floors rendered in a green-and-gold DOS dungeon style, with a living CRT glow
- Branching encounters with the **story beings** of the MindScape — what you say to them matters
- Turn-based combat with spells, relics, critical hits, and weapons you can see in your own hands
- Equipment, relics, charms, and shopkeepers across every floor
- An automap, an adaptive generative score that shifts with each world, and a full sound design
- Save and resume any time, right in your browser

## Secrets

MindScape keeps things back for those who look.

There are rooms that will not show themselves to a party that only walks the obvious path. There is more than one way for this story to end — the dungeon remembers how you treat the beings you meet, and the ending you're given reflects the road you took to reach it. Some of what's buried here is generous. Some of it is strange. A little of it is hidden well enough that most players will never see it at all.

None of it will be spoiled for you here. Explore everything, talk to everyone, search the walls that feel too quiet, and decide for yourself what kind of party you want to be. The world is listening.

---

## Tips for the road

- Talk to every being you find — and remember that the choices you make with them don't unmake themselves.
- A being who has nothing left to give will leave; that's a sign you've seen all of them, not a bug.
- The minimap marks seals, treasure, beings, the boss, and the way down. Watch the glowing markers.
- If the final door refuses you, it will tell you exactly what it still needs. Listen, then go find it.
- Equip the relics. Camp to recover. Spend your Thin Gold.
